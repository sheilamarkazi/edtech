Understanding Plagiarism

A Scavenger Hunt Activity

Learner Description: This activity is a refresher on plagiarism for students at Grayson County Career and Technical Center.

Introduction

Watch this video on the concept of plagiarism from a robot's point of view.


Instructions

  1. Open the Scavenger Hunt Worksheet and save it to your Google Drive.
  2. Find the answers to each hunt by reading the linked website content.
  3. Record your answers in the worksheet.
  4. When you are done, check your answers against the Scavenger Hunt Answer Key.

Hunt #1 - What is plagiarism?

Please hunt for the answers on this page: What is Plagiarism?

  1. Is turning in someone else's work as your own considered plagiarism?
  2. Do you need to give credit when citing someone else's words or ideas?
  3. What do you need to do to prevent plagiarism?
  4. Is using an image without permission or citation considered plagiarism?

Hunt #2 - Paraphrasing and Plagiarism

Please hunt for the answers on this page: Why is it still plagiarism when I paraphrase and use my own words?

  1. Do you need to provide citations for your own original ideas?
  2. Do you need to provide citations when you quote someone?
  3. Please define what it means to "paraphrase".
  4. If you paraphrase someone else's idea, do you still need to provide a citation?

Hunt #3 - Confidence and Plagiarism

Please hunt for the answers on this page: What Does Confidence Have to do with Plagiarism?

  1. Please list four reasons why students plagiarize.
  2. How can you increase your confidence and skill as a writer?
  3. What negative effects does plagiarism have on your confidence and your education?

Hunt #4 - Preventing Plagiarism

Please hunt for the answers on this page: Preventing Plagiarism when Writing

  1. If you are not sure as to whether an idea came from your or from another source, provide a citation. This includes any sources of inspiration or concepts that were critical to developing your ideas. Providing citations will strengthen your work in several ways. Please list at least two ways.
  2. True or False: If you are paraphrasing someone's work, you must change both the words and the sentence structure.
  3. If you have successfully changed both the wording and the sentence structure, do you still need to cite your source?
