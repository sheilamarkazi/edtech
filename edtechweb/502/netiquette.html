Netiquette and Machine Safety

Learner Description: These important safety considerations are for new Career Investigation students using the STEAM Lab at Grayson County Career and Technical Center.


The Most Important Thing

Keeping a safe environment is our number one concern.

In addition to physical safety, we will maintain an environment where everyone can feel safe and comfortable learning.

We will focus our efforts on being safe, courteous, and respectful.

Your Education, Your Safety

Whether your are taking Career Investigations or Engineering, you are welcome and strongly encouraged to use the computers and machines in the STEAM Lab. These tools are at your disposal, and your creativity is the limit.

When in the STEAM Lab, you must take measures to keep yourself safe, as well as protect the safety of those around you. The computers and the machines should be used responsibly.


Using the Internet

  • Keep your personal information limited

    • Personal information includes your birthdate, your address, and your credit card information, and more. If you're in doubt, please ask a responsible adult.

    Video - Personal and Private Information (1 min 36 sec)

  • Cyberbullying is not OK

    • If someone online is making you feel uncomfortable or weird, tell your instructor. Whether it is a stranger or another student, it is not normal for them to make you feel uncomfortable. To protect yourself and those around you, notify your instructor immediately.

    Video - What's Cyberbullying? (1 min 44 sec)

  • Don't talk with strangers

    • You never know who is behind the screen. If this is a risk you must take, proceed with caution. You never need to send pictures of yourself to strangers.

  • Keep your passwords safe and don't download things without permission

    Questions?

    You're already thinking of many exceptions to these general rules, and that's understandable. Every situation is unique. Whenever questions arise, please ask a responsible adult to discuss what's right for you.

Using the Machinery

You must first be trained by your instructor prior to using any machine. Speficially, this includes the laser cutter, CNC mill, 3D printer, and vinyl cutter. Training includes both how to use ay relevant design software, as well as operating the machinery. You may create your designs at any computing station. When it is time to use the machine, you must notify your instructor. Your instructor is responsible for providing the appropriate supervision and will provide any assistance you require while using the machinery.
