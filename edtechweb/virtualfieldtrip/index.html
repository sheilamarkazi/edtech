NASA Headquarters

NASA Headquarters, officially known as Mary W. Jackson NASA Headquarters or NASA HQ, is located in Washington, DC. It is a little over five hours away from Independence, VA.



NASA Headquarters houses the overseeing leadership responsible for directing NASA activities. "NASA explores the unknown in air and space, innovates for the benefit of humanity, and inspires the world through discovery" (source: nasa.gov).

NASA makes a huge impact on the economy. Such a big program requires a diverse team to operate. Not only scientists and engineers work at NASA. All types of roles, from accountants to artists, exist.

If you could make up your own job at NASA, what would it be? What would your responsibilities include? Watch the video below for some inspiration.

Goddard Space Flight Center

The Goddard Space Flight Center (GSFC) is located in Greenbelt, MD. Greenbelt is 30 minutes away from Washington DC, making the Goddard Space Flight Center quite close to NASA HQ. GSFC is a little over five hours away from Independence, VA.



The Goddard Space Flight Center is a major laboratory focused on developing and operating spacecraft. Activities at Goddard include managing communications with astronauts on the International Space Station and operating the Hubble Telescope.

Goddard, in cooperation with the U.S. Geological Survey, also manages the Landsat program. The Landsat program has been used to collect satellite imagery of the Earth since 1975. Landsat 9, the most recent satellite, was launched on September 27, 2021. The Landsat program provides critical data used for applications such as "agriculture, forestry, land use, water resources and natural resource exploration" (source: nasa.gov).

Watch the video below. Afterwards, ask yourself what information a farmer uses at their job. Which of that data could be collected from a satellite?

Langley Research Center

NASA's Langley Research Center is located in Hampton, VA, which is about 5 and a half hours away from Independence, VA. This region was originally settled by Native Americans and was first colonized in 1606.



NASA Langley is the oldest of NASA's field centers (source: nasa.gov). A field center is where NASA's work takes place or is led from. The focus at Langley is developing technology for space exploration and expanding our understanding of Earth's atmosphere (source: nasa.gov).

Some of the ongoing work at Langley is highlighted in their video library. Watch the two videos below. After watching them, think about the importance of each project. If you could be part of either project team, which project would you want to work on? Why?



Wallops Flight Facility

Wallops Flight Facility is located approximately seven hours away from Independence, VA. It is located on Wallops Island, off the shore of Virginia.



Wallops Flight Facility (WFF) is a rocket launch site. It is equipped to launch a variety of rockets spanning from "research aircraft, unmanned aerial systems and high-altitude balloons to suborbital and orbital rockets" (source: nasa.org). WFF provides flight and launch services to various sectors, not only serving NASA, but other government entities and commercial aerospace, as well.

Employees at WFF work in a wide range of environments. From office settings, to manufacturing floors, to flight facilities - different jobs require different work conditions. Watch the video below to tour the spaces at WFF. Afterwards, think about your ideal work conditions. What would you want your workplace to look, feel, and sound like?
