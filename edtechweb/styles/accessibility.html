What is Web Accessibility?

The goal of web accessibility is to promote equal access to a website's information.

Web accessibility is the pracice of minimizing barriers so that individuals with disabilities can interact with a web page. Disabilities can be permanent, temporary, or depend on the situation. Have you ever been in a loud classroom where you cannot hear the video in your assignment? That is an example of a situational disability. Video captioning can help you in this situationn.

Web accessibility practices can be used to address many needs such as:

  • Visual impairments
  • Hearing impairments
  • Limited mobility
  • Learning diffiulties, such as dyslexia and dyscalculia
  • And much more

Tools for Understanding

What is it like to interact with a website when you have a disability? It can be challenging to put ourselves in another person's shoes. Abilities have a huge range, as each person is unique. Here are just a few examples of how web information might be. experienced by different people with different abilities.

Cochlear Implant

Cochlear implants are devices that assist individuals with hearing loss. Cochlear implants help people hear. However, they have a unique sound quality. Listen to what cochlear implants sound like.

Color Blindness

Some people see color differently from most people. This can make it challenging to view information on a website. For example, some people see green and red as very similar colors. Look at examples of how color blindness can affect visual perception.

Imagine a website displaying green text on a red background. Some people would find that very hard to read!
